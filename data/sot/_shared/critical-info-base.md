## Health & Safety

This home is equipped with certified smoke detectors, CO2 detectors, a first aid kit and fire extinguishers for use in an emergency.

## Emergency Procedures — Fire

### Action in case of fire

1. Please remain calm
2. Touch your door. If hot, do NOT open
3. If you are able to leave your room, proceed to the nearest fire exit
4. Stay beneath the smoke by crawling on the floor if necessary
5. Upon exiting the building, proceed to the assembly area outside the property — see **Property-Specific Notes** for the assembly point
6. Call **111** to alert the fire brigade
7. Do not re-enter the building until authorised by your hosts

### If you must stay in your room

1. Stay calm
2. Fill the bathroom sink with water
3. Wet sheets and towels, seal these around the door
4. Call **111** to advise you are unable to evacuate your room

## Emergency Procedures — Earthquake

New Zealand sits on the boundary of two tectonic plates and earthquakes are part of life here. Most are minor; the official advice is **Drop, Cover, Hold**.

### During the shaking

1. **DROP** to your hands and knees — this prevents being knocked over
2. **COVER** your head and neck under a sturdy table, desk, or in an interior doorway
3. **HOLD** on until the shaking stops

If outside, move away from buildings, trees, power lines, and the lakefront. If in a vehicle, pull over (away from bridges, overpasses, and powerlines) and stay inside.

### After the shaking stops

1. **Check yourself and others for injuries.** Apply first aid; the kit location is in **Property-Specific Notes**.
2. **Smell for gas.** If you smell gas, **do NOT use any electrical switches or open flames**. Turn off the gas at the main shutoff valve — the location is in **Property-Specific Notes**. Open windows and leave the building.
3. **Check the building structure.** If you see major cracks, leaning walls, or fallen ceilings, evacuate to the assembly point and call **111**.
4. **Expect aftershocks.** Stay alert; further Drop/Cover/Hold may be needed.
5. **Tune in to Civil Defence updates** — mobile networks may be slow or down.

### Tsunami note (lake-side properties)

Lake Wakatipu itself is not a tsunami risk. NZ's tsunami risk is from large coastal earthquakes — for Queenstown, this is not relevant. If you are travelling outside the region and feel a strong or long earthquake near the coast, **move immediately to high ground** without waiting for an official warning.
