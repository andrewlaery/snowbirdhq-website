### Heating Guide

Snowbird sits in New Zealand's Southern Alps, where temperatures
swing dramatically (30°C+ to near-freezing). The home features a
**heated concrete floor slab** that holds a comfortable preset
temperature throughout. Wall radiators are integrated into this
whole-house heating system.

**Important — temperature changes take time.** Because heating runs
through the slab, adjustments take 2–4 hours to take effect. Plan
ahead — if you're cold, contact the host early so we can make
adjustments.

**Fireplace + underfloor interaction.** Using the wood burner in the
studio lounge causes the underfloor heating to reduce output in the
ground-floor bedrooms. If you use the fire in the evening, keep
bedroom doors closed to retain heat. After the fire goes out, the
underfloor system gradually rebalances (slowly, due to the slab).

**For best comfort.** Keep doors and windows closed during cold
periods to retain stored heat. Adjust your clothing for individual
preferences. Contact the host for any temperature adjustments.

**When leaving the house.** Close all doors and windows in cooler
weather to maintain temperature for your return.

**Top-floor master bedroom tips.** This room can be more challenging
to regulate as heat rises:
  - Close all bedroom doors (entrance and bathroom).
  - Close the atrium sliding window/door.
  - Open 1–3 windows as needed to fine-tune.
  - Use the provided floor fan for additional airflow.

**Wall radiators** are fully integrated with the whole-house system
and require no manual adjustment.

### Rubbish & Recycling

- Red bins (rubbish): collected every Wednesday.
- Blue bins (glass) and yellow bins (mixed recycling) alternate
  each Wednesday.
- Verify the schedule at
  https://www.qldc.govt.nz/services/rubbish-recycling/rubbish-recycling-collection/
  or check which bins the neighbours have out.
- Place bins on the street curb beside the driveway by 7am or the
  night before. Collect them off the street by Wednesday evening.
