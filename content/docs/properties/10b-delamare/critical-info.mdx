### Health & Safety

* This home is equipped with certified smoke detectors, a fire extinguisher, and a first aid kit for use in an emergency.
* **Instructions:**
    * First Aid Kit: in the kitchen (please re-stock or notify us if items are used).
    * Fire Extinguisher: under the kitchen sink.
    * Emergency Exit: in the event of an emergency, please exit the home through the front door. The emergency meeting point is at the bottom of the driveway, clear of the garage.

### Emergency Procedures - Fire

* De La Mare Views is fitted with smoke detectors throughout the house — please do not disconnect them.
* **Exits:**
    * Please familiarise yourself with your nearest fire exit.
    * Ground floor exits:
        * Front door
        * Back door (leading to outdoor clothes horse and garden)
        * Living-area sliding door to the deck
    * Upstairs: there is no separate fire exit upstairs — use the internal stairs to the front/back doors.
* **Action in case of fire:**
    * Please remain calm.
    * Touch your bedroom door. If hot, do NOT open.
    * If you can leave your room, proceed to the nearest fire exit.
    * Stay beneath the smoke by crawling on the floor if necessary.
    * Upon exiting the building, proceed to the assembly area at the bottom of the driveway.
    * Call 111 to alert the fire brigade, either from your phone or a neighbouring house.
    * Do not re-enter the building until authorised to do so.
* **How to survive if you have to stay in your room:**
    * Stay calm.
    * Fill the bathroom sink with water.
    * Wet sheets and towels, seal these around the door.
    * Call 111 to advise you are unable to evacuate your room.

### Hazards

* **Please be aware of the following hazards at De La Mare Views, which include but are not limited to:**
    * **Steep driveway:** The driveway can become icy and slippery in winter. Salt for de-icing is in a large white bag inside the garage — please use it.
    * **Internal stairs:** The house has two levels connected by carpeted stairs. Please supervise children carefully, especially young children, and take care going up or down the stairs.
    * **Elevated deck:** The outdoor deck has views over a drop — please supervise children on the deck.
    * **Garage door:** The garage door swings outward. Ensure no vehicle or person is directly in front before opening.
    * **Hot water cylinder:** Hot water is limited and can run out with simultaneous heavy use (showers + washing machine + kitchen taps). Reheat takes 40–60 minutes.

# Essentials & Other

### Insects

* Central Queenstown does not have sandflies (similar to mosquitos), however we do have small, non-harmful flying bugs which are attracted to light. If you would like to have your windows open in the evenings for ventilation, please ensure to turn out your lights or alternatively keep blinds closed.
* During the warmer months (November–February), seasonal insects may gather near ceilings — they are harmless.
* If you are travelling to the West Coast, the Glenorchy/Dart River area or Milford/Doubtful Sound, we recommend taking insect repellent.

### Power

* Generally all power sockets in New Zealand are 230/240 volts (50 Hz). If you are plugging in devices, please ensure they are rated accordingly.
* **International electronics** (high-wattage appliances, voltage converters) may trip the fuses. The fuse box is in the laundry room next to the kitchen.
