### Health & Safety

* The lodge is equipped with certified smoke detectors and a fire extinguisher for use in an emergency.
* **Emergency Exit:** In the event of an emergency, please exit the lodge through the front door. The emergency meeting point is outside, a safe distance from the building — the grass area opposite the house is a good place to assemble.

### Emergency Procedures - Fire

* The lodge is fitted with smoke detectors — please do not disconnect them.
* **Exits:**
    * Front door.
    * Back doors to the outdoor spa / fire pit area.
    * Sliding doors from the living/dining area.
* **Action in case of fire:**
    * Please remain calm.
    * Touch your bedroom door. If hot, do NOT open.
    * If you can leave your room, proceed to the nearest exit.
    * Stay beneath the smoke by crawling on the floor if necessary.
    * Assemble on the grass area opposite the house, a safe distance from the building.
    * Call 111 to alert the fire brigade.
    * Do not re-enter the building until authorised to do so.
* **How to survive if you have to stay in your room:**
    * Stay calm.
    * Fill the bathroom sink with water.
    * Wet sheets and towels, seal these around the door.
    * Call 111 to advise you are unable to evacuate your room.

### Hazards

* **Please be aware of the following hazards at Lakeview Lodge Lower:**
    * **Internal stairs** to the upper bedroom level — please supervise children carefully.
    * **Outdoor spa:** An unsupervised spa can be a drowning risk for young children. Please keep the spa cover on when not in use and supervise children closely.
    * **Outdoor fire pit:** Keep children away from the fire pit when lit. Do not leave the fire unattended. Ensure it is fully extinguished before heading inside.
    * **Gas cooktop:** Standard kitchen gas safety — ensure burners are fully off when not in use, and do not leave cooking unattended.
    * **Driveway:** Can be slippery after rain — please take care.

# Essentials & Other

### Insects

* Central Queenstown does not have sandflies, however we do have small non-harmful flying bugs attracted to light. If you would like to have your windows open for ventilation, please turn out your lights or keep blinds closed.
* If you are travelling to the West Coast, the Glenorchy/Dart River area or Milford/Doubtful Sound, we recommend taking insect repellent.

### Power

* Generally all power sockets in New Zealand are 230/240 volts (50 Hz). If you are plugging in devices, please ensure they are rated accordingly.
* **Laundry-specific note:** do NOT run the washer and dryer simultaneously — this trips the circuit. See User Instructions for details.
