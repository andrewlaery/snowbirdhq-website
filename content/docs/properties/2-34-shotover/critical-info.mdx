### Health & Safety

* The apartment is equipped with certified smoke detectors and a fire extinguisher for use in an emergency.
* **Emergency Exit:** In the event of an emergency, please exit the apartment through the front door and down the alleyway to Shotover Street. The emergency meeting point is outside on Shotover Street, a safe distance from the building.

### Emergency Procedures - Fire

* The apartment is fitted with smoke detectors — please do not disconnect them.
* **Exits:**
    * Front door → alleyway → Shotover Street.
    * There is no alternative fire exit from the apartment — please keep the path from the front door to the street clear.
* **Action in case of fire:**
    * Please remain calm.
    * Touch your bedroom door. If hot, do NOT open.
    * If you can leave your room, proceed to the front door and down the alleyway to Shotover Street.
    * Stay beneath the smoke by crawling on the floor if necessary.
    * Assemble on Shotover Street, a safe distance from the building.
    * Call 111 to alert the fire brigade, either from your phone or a shop on Shotover Street.
    * Do not re-enter the building until authorised to do so.
* **How to survive if you have to stay in your room:**
    * Stay calm.
    * Fill the bathroom sink with water.
    * Wet sheets and towels, seal these around the door.
    * Call 111 to advise you are unable to evacuate your room.

### Hazards

* **Please be aware of the following hazards at 2/34 Shotover Street:**
    * **Internal stairs:** Please supervise children carefully, especially overnight trips to the bathroom.
    * **No elevator:** The apartment is accessed by stairs from the alleyway — please use the handrail and take care, particularly with luggage.
    * **Busy street:** The apartment opens onto a lively part of central Queenstown. Please exercise normal urban-area care when exiting onto Shotover Street.
    * **Balcony:** Exposed to direct sun with no shade — can be warm. Take normal care with children on the balcony.

# Essentials & Other

### Insects

* Central Queenstown does not have sandflies (similar to mosquitos), however we do have small, non-harmful flying bugs which are attracted to light. If you would like to have your windows open in the evenings for ventilation, please ensure to turn out your lights or keep blinds closed.
* If you are travelling to the West Coast, the Glenorchy/Dart River area or Milford/Doubtful Sound, we recommend taking insect repellent.

### Power

* Generally all power sockets in New Zealand are 230/240 volts (50 Hz). If you are plugging in devices, please ensure they are rated accordingly.
* The fuse box is in the laundry room, behind the door. If the power trips, flip the switch up. If it trips repeatedly, please contact the host.
