### Heating Guide

Snowbird sits in New Zealand's Southern Alps, where temperatures
swing dramatically (30°C+ to near-freezing). The home features a
**heated concrete floor slab** that holds a comfortable preset
temperature throughout. Wall radiators are integrated into this
whole-house heating system.

**Important — temperature changes take time.** Because heating runs
through the slab, adjustments take 2–4 hours to take effect. Plan
ahead — if you're cold, contact the host early so we can make
adjustments.

**Fireplace + underfloor interaction.** Using the wood burner in the
studio lounge causes the underfloor heating to reduce output in the
ground-floor bedrooms. If you use the fire in the evening, keep
bedroom doors closed to retain heat. After the fire goes out, the
underfloor system gradually rebalances (slowly, due to the slab).

**For best comfort.** Keep doors and windows closed during cold
periods to retain stored heat. Adjust your clothing for individual
preferences. Contact the host for any temperature adjustments.

**When leaving the house.** Close all doors and windows in cooler
weather to maintain temperature for your return.

**Top-floor master bedroom tips.** This room can be more challenging
to regulate as heat rises:
  - Close all bedroom doors (entrance and bathroom).
  - Close the atrium sliding window/door.
  - Open 1–3 windows as needed to fine-tune.
  - Use the provided floor fan for additional airflow.

**Wall radiators** are fully integrated with the whole-house system
and require no manual adjustment.

### Gas Fire (Upstairs Lounge)

Not recommended as a primary heat source — use as needed while
you're in the upstairs lounge. The gas fire is remote-controlled.
Remote is on the wall in the same room.

1. Press the **top button** on the black switch directly under the
   remote (this powers the gas fire).
2. Press **Power** on the remote. Confirm the temperature appears
   on the digital display (if not, press Power again).
3. The fireplace takes a few seconds to ignite.
4. The gas fire is on a **60-minute timer** — repeat the steps to
   reignite if needed.
5. Always turn the gas fire off after use.

### Studio Wood Burner (Lounge / Dining Room)

- Lift the handle up to open and close the fire door.
- Light the fire with the fire-lighters provided in the wood box.
- Leave the door slightly open while the fire establishes and the
  glass warms (avoids condensation build-up).
- Close the door once the fire is established and lock it back into
  place.
- Do not leave the door open — risk of over-firing damages the
  appliance.
- Use the supplied baskets to collect firewood / kindling stored
  in the garage.
- Open the door only by touching the wooden knob on the front.
- Do not empty ash into the rubbish bins or any area exposed to
  high winds.
- If excess ash builds up, contact the hosts.

### Spa Control Panel

- Temperature is preset for comfort and safety.
- Press **JETS** to turn jets on/off.
- Press **LIGHTS** to turn underwater lights on/off.
- **If the panel appears locked** (padlock icon, unresponsive
  buttons): hold **OPTIONS + LIGHTS** together for 3–5 seconds.

### Rubbish & Recycling

- Red bins (rubbish): collected every Wednesday.
- Blue bins (glass) and yellow bins (mixed recycling) alternate
  each Wednesday.
- Verify the schedule at
  https://www.qldc.govt.nz/services/rubbish-recycling/rubbish-recycling-collection/
  or check which bins the neighbours have out.
- Place bins on the street curb beside the driveway by 7am or the
  night before. Collect them off the street by Wednesday evening.

### Bosch Gas Hob

Press in and rotate the corresponding dial on the oven front until the
hob ignites. Adjust as needed for the correct heat output.

### Insinkerator (Food Waste Disposal)

Press the silver button on the right-hand side of the tap to use the
food waste disposal system. Ensure water is running for smooth
operation.

This is an **air button** — if water from the sink gets into the
button it can limit operation. The button self-corrects after a few
hours.

### AEG Washing Machine + Dryer

**AEG Washing Machine (bottom appliance).**
- Turn on with the **On/Off** button.
- Detergent goes in the tray on the left-hand side.
- Select the preferred wash cycle using the wheel.
- Press **Start/Pause**.
- Please leave the washer door open when not in use.
- Laundry powder is provided under the laundry sink.

**AEG Dryer (top appliance).**
- Turn on with the **On/Off** button.
- Select the drying program using the wheel.
- Select the dryness level.
- Press **Start/Pause**.
- Every time you use the dryer, check / clean the lint filter inside
  the bottom of the dryer door.
