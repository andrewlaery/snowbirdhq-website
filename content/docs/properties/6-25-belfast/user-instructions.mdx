### Heating Guide

#### Main Floor (Kitchen, Living & Master Bedroom areas)
##### **Overview**
- SkyCrest sits in New Zealand's Southern Alps, where temperatures can swing dramatically (from 30°C+ to near freezing)
- The house features a heated concrete floor slab that maintains a comfortable preset temperature throughout
- The temperature of the house is set for comfortable general purpose. Clothing should be used to control your personal temperature.
##### **For Best Comfort**
- Keep doors and windows closed during cold periods to maintain the heat stored in the concrete slab
- Adjust your clothing for individual temperature preferences
- *Please contact your host if you need any temperature adjustments.
##### **Gas Fire**
- Not recommended as a primary heat source
- Use only as needed while you're in the upstairs lounge
* The Gas fire is remote controlled. The remote is found on the table in the same room.
* **Instructions:**
	* Press the Power button on the remote. Ensure after you press the power button, you can see the temperate on the digital display of the remote (if not, press the power button again).
	* The fireplace will takes a few seconds to respond and ignite.
	* Please ensure you turn off the gas fire after use.
#### Top Floor (TV Nock, Bedroom 2 & 3 areas)

* There are 3 air-conditioning heat pumps (Daikin CTXM25RVMA) for use. You will find the remote controls for them on the walls.

#### When Leaving the House
- Close all doors and windows during cooler weather to maintain ideal temperature for your return

#### ➡️ Alpine Saunas Barrel Sauna with Harvia Vega Sauna Heater (BC60)

**Location:** Outdoor area
**Description:** Outdoor barrel-style wooden sauna with panoramic windows
##### **🔥 Basic Operations**
###### **Preparing the Sauna**
- Turn on the sauna **30–45 minutes before use**.
- Inside the sauna, there are **two control dials**:
    - **Temperature Dial**
        - Controls the heat level.
        - For normal use, turn this **to HIGH**.
    - **Timer Dial**
        - Controls when and how long the heater runs.
        - **Turn the timer into the WHITE zone** to start the sauna heating immediately.
        - ⚠️ If the dial is turned **past the white zone into the BLACK zone**, this sets a **start delay**.
		- This is useful if you are heading out and want the sauna to **start heating later**, so it’s ready when you return.
- The sauna typically reaches **80–90°C** within **15–30 minutes** once started.
- Ensure the sauna door is closed properly to maintain heat.
- Have water available for hydration.
######  **Adding Steam (Optional)**
- Pour a **small amount of water** over the heated stones using the ladle.
- Add water **gradually** to avoid sudden steam bursts.
- Never pour excessive water at once.
######  **Using the Sauna**
- Enter once the sauna has reached a comfortable temperature.
- Sit on a towel for hygiene.
- Limit initial sessions to **10–15 minutes**.
- Exit immediately if you feel dizzy or unwell.
- **Please turn the Sauna off** once you have finished using it.
